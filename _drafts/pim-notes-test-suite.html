Okay, I can't find a category for this, so it officially goes in The Attic.

I've lost one of the best PDAs I ever had: my Treo. I've traded it for a Samsung Galaxy S, loaded with lots of AT&T crapware. It's neat, but it's missing the Personal Information Management (PIM) functions that a PDA should have, and that I really need.

So I'm looking for apps that can replace the PIM functionality I lost. I need integrated contacts, calendar, and notes. I know lots of other people need tasks and expense tracking, but I don't: tasks always wind up as calendar events, and if you need an expense program, you should probably just give up and go get a Blackberry.

I'm starting off with the note-taking functionality. I intend to try a bunch of apps and pick the one that gets the job done best. While I'm doing all that testing, I'll write down what I find for my future reference, and as a bonus, you get to benefit from my experiences!

I'm starting with the note-taking functionality. To keep things fair, I'm figuring out my rating scale and testing methods before I actually go rating anything.

What does a note program have to do? Obviously, take notes. And let you read them later. No biggie, right? This should be easy.

  1. Filtering (10 points)

    Oh, right. It would be nice to arrange the notes in small groups. Otherwise we'd be facing a sea of notes with no way to get the data we want back out.

    I like tags, because a single note can go in more than one place. Other methods of filtering I can think of are folders (which implies a file system) and categories (which are like tags, but only one per note).

    Note that, unless it can limit the display according to this attribute, it's not really filtering; so if you can apply tags, but you can't look at all the notes tagged "Personal", it doesn't count. Additionally, if it can't display notes without an attribute, it's not filtering either.

    Score
    Filter by tags: 10 points
    Filter by categories: 5 points
    Filter by folders: 5 points
    No filtering: 0 points

    There are other methods of filtering, some of which are occasionally useful. If the note app does any of these, give it some extra points:

    Bonus Score
    Filter by date: +1 point
    Filter by multiple attributes: +1 points
    Filter with boolean expressions: +2 points

  2. Quick Note (20 points)

    Lots of the note-taking I do is simple, quick little notes: a Wi-Fi network ID and WEP password, a vocabulary word to look up later, a passing idea...

    Starting from the home screen, measure how long it takes to enter this note: 

    
Roku hostname: rokutastic
Tivo hostname: dvr

    Score
    Under 20 seconds: 20 points
    20-40 seconds: 15 points
    40-60 seconds: 10 points
    60 or more seconds: 5 points
